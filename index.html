ChicGrasp

Diffusion Policy for Dual-Jaw Robotic Grasping

ChicGrasp is a robotics research project that explores imitation learning using a diffusion policy to control a UR10e robotic arm equipped with a customized two-DOF gripper. This system learns to manipulate real-world objects‚Äîin this case, chickens‚Äîby mimicking human demonstrations.

(Left) The robotic gripper equipped with two jaws and wrist cameras designed for autonomous poultry handling. (Right) Comparison between the current industry-standard manual process and our autonomous approach using imitation learning to pick up and rehang poultry.

üöÄ Highlights

  • Uses Diffusion Policy for continuous action prediction
  • Supports real-time control with a SpaceMouse interface
  • Trained on real human demonstrations with a custom gripper
  • Tested in industrial scenarios involving poultry handling

üß† Method

We leverage the power of diffusion models to learn a robust and flexible control policy from demonstrations. The dual-jaw gripper adds complexity in manipulation, and GraspDiff accounts for it by modeling an 8-dimensional action space: 6-DOF robot pose + 2-DOF gripper status.
